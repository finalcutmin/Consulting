About Us

Paula Barbag, MBA, CFRE

Founder and President

Paula Barbag, MBA, CFRE, Founder and President of Consulting For A Cause, is an accomplished professional with 30 years business experience in fundraising, marketing and human resources. Ms. Barbag is a Certified Fundraising Executive (CFRE) with a proven track record in raising significant and sustainable financial resources for nonprofit organizations. She possesses broad leadership expertise within startup, turnaround challenges and rapid growth situations.

Barbag is currently the Director of Development for RSDSA (Reflex Sympathetic Dystrophy Syndrome Association). RSDSA is a national organization that promotes public and professional awareness of Complex Regional Pain Syndrome (CRPS), also known as Reflex Sympathetic Dystrophy (RSD); raises funds to support medical research and provides advocacy and support for individuals and their loved ones.

For 23 years she was employed by Richmond Community Services, a not-for-profit organization serving children and adults with developmental disabilities and complex medical needs As Director of Marketing and Development, she developed an extensive donor base of corporations, foundations and individuals to support Richmond&rsquo;s programs and services. Ms. Barbag was awarded more than a million dollars in grants to support quality of life initiatives, capital improvement projects, workforce development, and a Volunteer and Community Outreach Program. She raised hundreds of thousands of dollars through coordinating successful fundraising events and major gifts.

Prior to working in development, Ms. Barbag was the Director of Human Resources for Richmond Community Services.

Ms. Barbag is currently enrolled in a Master's of Fundraising Management Program at Columbia University. She possesses a Bachelor of Science Degree in Business Management and a Masters of Business Administration in Personnel and Industrial Relations from Fairleigh Dickinson University. Ms. Barbag has a Professional Certificate in Philanthropy and Fundraising from New York University. She currently serves as Co-President of the Association of Development Officers and she is a member of the Association of Fundraising Professionals. Ms. Barbag served on the Board of Directors of the Westchester Human Resources Management Association and Search for Change.

Aaron Lapp

Vice President

Aaron Lapp, Vice President of Consulting for a Cause (CFC), is a fund development professional with 17 years of experience in non-profit fundraising and marketing. His expertise includes annual giving, major donor cultivation and solicitation as well as corporate and institutional giving programs and capital campaign management.

For the past 5 years, Mr. Lapp has been the Director of Development at Project Morry, a year-round youth development organization that provides at-risk children with an extraordinary summer camp experience and school year academic enrichment. As Development Director he successfully raised annual revenue by 12% which included a 65% increase in foundation support. He managed a portfolio of over 100 major donors and completed a successful $6 Million dollar capital campaign. Mr. Lapp provided management and oversight for the fund development functions of the organization including donor cultivation and solicitation, recognition, grant writing and special events oversight and management.

Prior to joining the Project Morry team, Mr. Lapp was the Director of Development and Public Relations at the Cenacle Retreat Center. While there he successfully raised annual giving by 40% through special events, individual giving programs, planned giving education and solicitation and an annual direct mail program. He also managed their major gifts portfolio and supplemented their "Volunteer Corps"through recruitment and training of new volunteers

Prior to his work at the Cenacle, Mr. Lapp was the Creative Services and Projects Manager with RKG Fundraising Services. His accomplishments included the design of award-winning direct mail programs based on comprehensive market research and competitive-intelligence gathering, which lead to revenue growth rates in combined excess of ten million dollars. He assisted in the development of donor enrollment and lapsed donor programs and an innovative donor cultivation program. Foremost, he successfully increased donor retention rates and expanded donor bases through expert relationship-building and a commitment to a proactive, solution-focused approach.

Mr. Lapp is the current Co-Vice President of Membership for the Association of Development Officers and a member of the Association of Fundraising Professionals. He holds a Bachelor of Science Degree from the State University College at Buffalo.

Lindsay Mitcher

Account Executive

Lindsay Mitcher, Account Executive for Consulting For A Cause, is a professional dedicated to creating a shared value across the profit and nonprofit sectors through philanthropy, partnership development, community engagement and advocacy. Lindsay specializes in developing integrated marketing communication strategies for non-profits proven to increase awareness and further advance an organization's mission.

Lindsay currently works in Marketing and Development at Richmond Community Services, a non-profit organization serving children and adults with significant developmental disabilities and complex medical needs. She expertly generates and analyzes donor giving capacity and donation history reports used to strategically increase the level of support from current donors. Lindsay conducts extensive prospect research that has directly led to the acquisition of major donors and both corporate and foundation grants. She has a proven track record of coordinating special events that support donor creation and retention while exceeding fundraising expectations. Lindsay established and manages Richmond's social media presence enabling the organization to stay connected to its constituents, while increasing awareness and support for Richmond's mission.

A graduate of the University at Buffalo Lindsay holds a Bachelors degree in Business Administration, with a concentration in Marketing. She has a Professional Certificate in Fundraising and Philanthropy from New York University. Lindsay serves on the board of the Nanuet Community Ambulance Corps., where she volunteers as an emergency medical technician.
